About me

I'm a Mexican mathematician and AI researcher based in Munich, Germany. I hold a PhD in Mathematics from LMU Munich (supervised by Prof. Gitta Kutyniok) and dual BSc degrees in Mathematics and Physics from UNAM, both with near-perfect GPAs.

My research bridges applied harmonic analysis, deep learning, and inverse problems — with applications in 3D computer vision, biomedical imaging, and computational physics. I currently work in the Computer Vision Group at TUM with Prof. Daniel Cremers.

Beyond academia, I co-founded AngioWave Imaging, a biomedical startup at the intersection of AI and vascular imaging, and am building a new venture in stealth. Previously, I led AI at NAZCA, a leading LatAm VC fund.
